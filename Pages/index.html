Yellow Movement AAU

The Yellow movement Addis Ababa University is an initiative started by Addis Ababa University School of Law lecturer and a group of students from the same school. It was established in 2011 with the aim of creating continuous discussions on issues of Gender Based Violence and empowerment of women. Even though student-led clubs are common in many higher educational institutions, this initiative and the gender inclusive agendas it explores are the first for the country. The initiative has over 30 core members, both female and male, and thousands of volunteers that engage with the team online and off.

Online Campaigns

Pagume Activism(ሴታዊ ጳጉሜ)

The pagume activism took the ET social media by the storm. The activism is an online campaign with a transformative objective to challenge attitudes towards various types of violence against women and question the state of gender inequality in Ethiopia. It is designed to record stories of sexism faced on a daily basis by ordinary women in ordinary places.

Fill The Shelf

The campaign worked on filling the shelf of Association for Women's Sanctuary and Development (AWSAD’s) empty library with books donated by supporters and students all around. AWSAD provides a shelter to survivors of GBV, usually mothers and their children and at times minors who have survived GBV attacks. Hence to support the educational facilities of the shelter, our movement worked on providing books and stationary materials for three consecutive years.

Justice For Hana
